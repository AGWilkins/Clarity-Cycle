About Andrew Wilkins

Andrew has spent more than thirty years making decisions in environments where being wrong is expensive.

His career has taken him from the trading floors of London and Chicago to hedge funds, investment banks, risk management, market surveillance and regulatory consulting.

Along the way he became increasingly interested in a simple question:

Why do smart people make poor decisions?

The answer rarely seemed to be intelligence.

More often it was attention.

People missed things. They reacted automatically. They became trapped by assumptions they didn't realise they were making.

Over time Andrew became fascinated by the mechanics of thinking itself.

Not what people think.
How they think.

Alongside his professional work he spent decades studying decision-making, performance, attention, pattern recognition and human behaviour. He has trained in martial arts for more than fifty years and has long been interested in the relationship between perception, judgement and action under pressure.

Clarity Cycle grew from a simple observation:

Most people train their bodies.

Very few people ever train how they think.

School teaches subjects.
University teaches subjects.
Most careers teach subjects.

Almost nobody teaches the cognitive skills that sit underneath them.

Attention.
Pattern recognition.
Decision-making.
Cognitive endurance.

Clarity Cycle was created to train those skills through short daily exercises that fit into real life.

Five minutes.
One drill.
Different angle each day.

Andrew lives in Essex with his wife Sadie and continues to write, build, experiment with AI and ask awkward questions.

Small practice. Compounding effect.
